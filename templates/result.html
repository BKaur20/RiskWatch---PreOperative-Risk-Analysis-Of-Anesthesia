Some general suggestions are:

Due Risk

Although the patient's parameters are within the normal range, its important to note that while there isn't any additional risk currently, unexpected complications could still occur. Therefore, it is recommended to thoroughly review the patient's medical history and engage in a comprehensive discussion to address any potential concerns.

Low Risk

Follow the standard preoperative procedures and routine protocols and keep monitoring any changes in the patient's health. Make sure that the patient knows about the anesthesia process and be ready to change anesthesia plans if something unexpected happens.

Moderate Risk

Moderate Risk: Consider doing additional assessments and consultations to gather more information about the risks. Use this information to make a specialized plan for the anesthesia that fits the patient better.

High Risk

Have a thorough talk with the patient and the healthcare team about the pros and cons of going ahead with anesthesia. Develop a personalized anesthetic plan considering the specific risks that the patient might have. Work closely with other specialists and have backup plans ready in case there are unexpected problems.

Disclaimer**: These suggestions are meant to guide an anesthesiologist in tailoring their approach based on the perceived level of risk. It's crucial for the anesthesiologist to use their professional judgment and collaborate with the broader healthcare team to ensure the best possible outcome for the patient.
